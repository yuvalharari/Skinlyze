How to Take a Good Photo

Follow these guidelines for the most accurate results

Good Lighting Use natural daylight or bright indoor light. Avoid flash glare.
Fill the Frame The lesion should be centered and clearly visible. Get close enough.
Flat Angle Hold the camera directly above the lesion, not at an angle.
Sharp Focus Ensure the image is in sharp focus. Tap the lesion on your phone screen to focus.
Critical: The background must be skin only - no clothing, surfaces, or external objects. This is essential for accurate segmentation and analysis.
